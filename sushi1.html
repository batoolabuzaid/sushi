The History of Sushi

More than 25+ years of experience

Sushi began as a method of preserving fish in fermented rice in Southeast Asia and was introduced to Japan in the 8th century. During the Edo period, it evolved into its modern form with the creation of "nigiri sushi, " where fresh fish slices are placed on small rice balls. As Japan opened up in the 19th century, sushi spread globally, gaining popularity in the U.S. with innovations like the California Roll. Today, sushi is beloved worldwide as a symbol of traditional Japanese cuisine..
